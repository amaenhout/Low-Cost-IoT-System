The Project

Our society is at a turning point where the world around us is getting deeply embedded with smart objects that are wireless inter-connected through the Internet. The term, Internet of Things, was introduced for the first time by Kevin Ashton in 2009. Different sensors, software, electronics and Internet connectivity are the enablers for networks of physical objects or things to transform these objects so they can capture and exchange data within the network. Recent studies and reports have shown the unprecedented popularity and spread of IoT project. A report by Mckinsey estimates the economic impact of the IoT from the current $3.9 trillion to $11.1 trillion a year by 2025. This significant economic impact is a direct result of connecting over 50 billion devices to the Internet at the end of 2020. One part of this growth concentrates on connecting everyday objects that are being used by humans. These internet-connected objects or IoT devices offer huge potential within various sectors. Examples of such applications are home automation and security, healthcare, smart vehicles, etc. While these applications are booming the recent years, the part of IoT involves connecting machines to the internet and with each other. M2M communication consists of embedding ICT hardware and software to Industry machinery. IoT, is seen as one of the fundamental technologies that brought the Fourth Revolution to the world around the 2000s. In 2011 Germany created a new strategy where they opted that their industries would change their business processes to more internet-connected environments; the Industry 4.0. As of this huge potential for IoT applications, more and more technologies are emerging, remarkable is the reduce cost prices for each of these technologies. Since individual projects are rising, we see that especially open-source hardware, software and communication methods are increasing like never before.
